\section{Calculations}

Taking into account $U_{CE}=4\,\text{V}$ and $I_C=I_B=15\,\mu\text{A}$:
\begin{align}
    h_{11} &= \frac{\Delta U_{BE}}{\Delta I_B} \big|_{U_{CE}=4\,\text{V}} \\
    \Delta I_B &= 19.89 - 9.96 = 9.13\,\mu\text{A} \\
    \Delta U_{BE} &= 0.622 - 0.603 = 19\,\text{mV} \\
    h_{11} &= \frac{19\,\text{mV}}{9.13\,\mu\text{A}} = 2.08\,\text{k}\Omega
\end{align}

In reverse characteristics:

$$h_{12}=\frac{\Delta U_{BE}}{\Delta U_{CE}}\big|_{I_B=15\mu A}=\frac{0.611-0.614}{10-0.3}=-0.0003$$

Transfer characteristics:

$$h_{21}=\frac{\Delta I_C}{\Delta I_B}\big|_{U_{CE}=4V}=\frac{2.55-1.207}{19.89-9.96}=\frac{1.343mA}{9.13\mu A}=135.24$$

Output characteristics:

$$h_{22}=\frac{\Delta I_C}{\Delta U_{CE}}\big|_{I_B=15\mu A} = \frac{(1.909-1.9)\cdot 10^{-3}}{5-3}=4.5\mu S$$

$$u_{in}=u_{BE}=2.08k\Omega\cdot 9.13[\mu A]-0.000309\cdot 4=0.0178V$$

$$i_{out}=i_C=h_21$$
